The reliable version of this workflow starts before editing.

Most timing problems come from source footage that was recorded against different references. Keep the capture simple, then the edit becomes a repeatable stack instead of a rescue job.

  1. Record one guide take

    Use a main vocal, instrument, click, backing track, or rehearsal take as the timing reference.

  2. Record overdubs against that same guide

    Use headphones so the guide does not leak into every phone recording unless you want it there.

  3. Add one clear cue

    A clap, count-in, lyric, entrance, or first note gives every take a shared moment to line up.

  4. Keep phone clips vertical for 9:16 output

    Portrait source clips make the final TikTok, Reels, and Shorts export easier to frame.

  5. Stack, balance, preview, and export

    Arrange the grid, balance each take, check sync by ear, then export a 1080 x 1920 MP4.
